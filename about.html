About

TL;DR: Behind the computer, I'm a GIS Analyst with a developer focus.

I currently work for the Tahoe Regional Planning Agency building digital mapping applications and data services. I'm also pursuing an M.S. in Cartography, GIS, and Web Map Programming from the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

Away from the computer, I enjoy exploring the outdoors (skiing, hiking, kayaking, mountain biking), traveling, and hanging out with my wife and dog.

I'm a native Wisconsinite, raised in Spring Green, WI, but now call South Lake Tahoe, CA home.

Background: I discovered "GIS" during my sophmore year at the University of Wisconsin - Milwaukee while visiting the American Geographical Society Library. There, I secured a position filing maps away, reshelving old National Geographics, and cataloging geospatial data. From there I was hooked. I knew I had found my lifes work, so I took every GIS class that UWM had to offer, both in the Geography and Urban Planning Departments, and spent an inordinate amount of time tinkering in ArcMap.

In the summer of 2007, I landed an internship with the City of New Berlin, that allowed me to apply my newly acquried GIS knowledge. It was an amazing experience working on projects across the Public Works, Engineering and Community Developmeent departments. I was exposed to countless GIS projects. The internship evovled into a year round-full time GIS Specialist job.

I had an itch to travel and broaden my GIS experience, so I jumped at the chance to map black bear home ranges in Sequoia and Kings Canyone National Parks in CA. This experience changed my view of the world.
